Libra Cryptocurrency

Developed in relative secrecy over the past year, Libra is a new cryptocurrency from... Facebook. Obvious concerns first, Libra will be controlled by a Swiss-based non-profit, overseen by other companies, with Facebook's involvement handled by a subsidiary that is not allowed to share financial data with other divisions. It will be backed by real-world currencies like the US dollar, Euro, and Yen, and, if it works as intended, will become a worldwide currency without the fees and delays common with traditional banking. 27 partners, including Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Uber, and Vodafone have signaled their support so far, with hundreds more expected before the first Libra coins make their way to the public in 2020.

    Motor Synth

    There are a ton of synthesizers out there, so it's notable when something truly new appears. Motor Synth is nothing if not novel. It produces sounds by spinning eight brushless electromotors at precise RPMs that correspond to musical notes. Magnetic pickups turn the resulting oscillations into sound. At the same time, infrared sensors read the reflective optical disks on the shafts of the motors, converting their readings into sound. Its old-school layout also features common analog controls as well as an innovative looping system, and in addition to MIDI control, the Synth can also track a mono signal from its 1/4-inch input, turning it into a harmonizer for an electric guitar. A protective glass cover sits above the motors, making the instrument as interesting to look at as it is to hear.

    Pre-Order from Indiegogo / $749+

    Winston Online Privacy Device

    There's no more contentious topic in tech than privacy. While large corporations hide behind half-statements and legalese, the truth is there's little way for regular people to know how much of their online activity is being tracked. With the Winston Online Privacy Device, the answer is nearly zero. This small, simple box sits between your modem and router, and routes all traffic through 20-30 other Winston units, choosing new connections randomly every hour. As a result, your data is scrambled, preventing traditional tracking techniques, and by blocking over 90,000 trackers, ads, and clickbait sources, it can actually speed up your browsing and reduce data usage. Its browser-accessed dashboard lets you see what's being blocked, and each one comes with a free one-year subscription to the service.

    Pre-order from Kickstarter / $189+

    NASA Graphics Standards Manual

    For those that grew up in late '70s and '80s, the graphic designs of NASA are some of the most memorable of the period, appearing on everything from hats to the Space Shuttle. This reprinting of the NASA Graphics Standards Manual celebrates this outstanding identity system, which was used from 1975 to 1992. Recreated for public use for the first time from the personal copies of the original designers, Richard Danne and Bruce Blackburn, it covers the logo, placement, color schemes, and other design standards that helped capture the imagination of a generation and make the American space agency a lasting, iconic part of our legacy of exploration. Included are scans of Danne's personal copy of the original manual, a forward from Danne, an essay by Christopher Bonanos, reproductions of the original NASA 35mm slide presentation, and scans of the 'Managers Guide', a follow up booklet distributed by NASA. Each copy is individually wrapped in a foil static shield, adding even more nostalgic rocket-age flair to this iconic design reference.

    220 pages / 129 Image Plates / 9.5" x 11.5" / Hardcover

    Buy From Uncrate Supply / $79
