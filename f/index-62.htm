Audemars Piguet Royal Oak Perpetual Calendar Ultra-Thin Watch

It's funny that the world's thinnest perpetual calendar might have the longest name. Based on Audemars Piguet's RD#2 concept, the 41mm, titanium and platinum case of the Royal Oak Perpetual Calendar Ultra-Thin is just 6.3mm thick. The Calibre 5133 movement that powers it is just 2.89mm thick, so as to make room for the satin blue dial with white gold markers, lumed white gold hands, and the various sub-dials indicating the day, date, astronomical moon, month, leap year, and day/night. It's finished with a matching titanium bracelet and is available exclusively at Audemars Piguet boutiques.

    Zenith El Primero A384 Revival Watch

    Created to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the El Primero — the movement many consider to be the world's first automatic chronograph — Zenith is releasing the final installment of three special timepieces. Aptly named the A384 Revival after the groundbreaking watch, the new model was created using a reverse engineering approach where the original was digitized via laser and scanned to produce the most accurate 3D concept. In addition to the 37mm matte-finished steel case and lacquered white and black tachymeter dial, the Revival adds upgraded protection in the form of sapphire crystal, an exhibition caseback, and the brand new El Primero 400 movement with a 50-hour power reserve. The A384 will be available this fall with either a black leather strap with rubber lining or with an integrated metal ladder bracelet option — another homage to the original.

    NASA Graphics Standards Manual

    For those that grew up in late '70s and '80s, the graphic designs of NASA are some of the most memorable of the period, appearing on everything from hats to the Space Shuttle. This reprinting of the NASA Graphics Standards Manual celebrates this outstanding identity system, which was used from 1975 to 1992. Recreated for public use for the first time from the personal copies of the original designers, Richard Danne and Bruce Blackburn, it covers the logo, placement, color schemes, and other design standards that helped capture the imagination of a generation and make the American space agency a lasting, iconic part of our legacy of exploration. Included are scans of Danne's personal copy of the original manual, a forward from Danne, an essay by Christopher Bonanos, reproductions of the original NASA 35mm slide presentation, and scans of the 'Managers Guide', a follow up booklet distributed by NASA. Each copy is individually wrapped in a foil static shield, adding even more nostalgic rocket-age flair to this iconic design reference.

    220 pages / 129 Image Plates / 9.5" x 11.5" / Hardcover

    Timex American Documents Watch

    One of America's signature brands dives back into domestic production with this made in the U.S.A. timepiece. Inspired by the people, culture, and landscape of the country, each American Documents watch is made by American craftsmen with American materials and a high-quality Swiss movement. The watch features a case forged from stainless steel and hand finished to a brushed satin with a polished top ring. The caseback has a brass insert that depicts the continental U.S., while Alaska and Hawaii are stamped on the black leather strap. Impact-resistant Gorilla Glass protects the minimalist white dial, a seconds sub-register, date window, and gold-plated Swiss movement.     Harley-Davidson LiveWire Electric Motorcycle

    With a muscular shape and aggressive stance, the Harley-Davidson LiveWire is unlike anything that's come before. The LiveWire is the latest example of the innovative spirit of the most iconic motorcycle brand in the world. Its permanent magnetic electric motor provides instant torque, so a flick of the wrist takes you from 0 to 60 in 3.0 seconds without any of the shifting or clutch work required by its combustion-based cousins. The motor sits below the battery to lower the center of gravity for easier handling, a fully adjustable Showa suspension helps improve the ride further, ABS and Traction Control for enhanced cornering and Brembo brakes ensure you can get it stopped in a hurry.     Laurent Ferrier Tourbillon Grand Sport Watch

    Before he put his mark on independent watchmaking, Laurent Ferrier was a racer, competing in the 24 Hour Le Mans multiple times. It was there that he'd meet his friend and future business partner François Servanin. In honor of their 40-year friendship comes the '70s racing-inspired Tourbillon Grand Sport. The smoked taupe dial contrasts with the orange luminescent hands and indexes to give it a sporty appearance, while the Tourbillon movement with Swiss lever escapement keeps things running smoothly. The 44mm stainless steel case has a spherical tinted sapphire crystal, is water resistant to 100m, and is finished with a taupe rubber strap. Limited to just 12 numbered examples.
