    Ducati Streetfighter V4 Motorcycle

    In 2017, Ducati unveiled the Panigale V4 to the public. A thinly-disguised MotoGP prototype for the street, the V4 packed a MotoGP-derived 1103cc V4 engine making 214 horsepower — the most in its class — and out-performing everything else in any category you wanted to measure. Taken as it was from Ducati's MotoGP program, the Panigale V4 also had a MotoGP-like riding position, which was the only thing to possibly critique about the bike. Now you can't even say that with the upcoming release of the Streetfighter V4. Using the same chassis and engine as the Panigale, the Streetfighter loses the coverings to show off the beautiful motor and gets a more upright riding position with higher handlebars. Nearly everything else is carried over, which means that not only will the Streetfighter V4 dust everything else on the road, you can be a lot more comfortable while doing it. The official debut will take place at the EIMCA show later in 2019.

    Recast Moto Guzzi Nevada 750 Motorcycle

    Founded in Belarus in 2014, Recast Moto creates custom motorcycles influenced by their home country. Matiz Lemark of Recast took a rough 1999 Moto Guzzi Nevada 750 cruiser and turned it into the blacked out scrambler you see here. The design revolves around a vintage Honda CB750 gas tank that was sourced from the US and took nearly a month to get to Minsk. In the meantime, Recast took the opportunity to strip the Guzzi down for painting, rebuilding the motor and adding spoked wheels with knobby all-terrain tires. Custom controls were made from duralumin and the grips were sourced from a snowmobile — a far more common vehicle in Belarus. A hand-stitched leather skateboard seat, custom subframe, and several coats of charcoal black finished the sinister project.

    Nmoto Nostalgia BMW R7 Motorcycle

    In 1934, BMW debuted the R7 concept motorcycle during the height of the Art Deco style. The R7 was never made but would inspire the later R17 and R5 models. Thought to be lost until 2005, the R7 was rediscovered and fully restored to its former glory. That is the story behind Nmoto's Nostalgia R7, an exacting recreation of the stunning original built on BMW's current R nineT. Every detail from the R7 is there, from the partially-open fenders to the fish-fin exhaust pipes, wire wheels, and headlight-speedometer combo. The engine is enclosed in aerodynamic panels with exposed cylinder heads, all covered in black with white pinstriping the highlight the flow of the bodywork. Provide your own R nineT and you can walk away with one of the best custom motorcycles on the road.
