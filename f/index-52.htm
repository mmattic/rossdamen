Adler Lodge Ritten

Situated on the Renon plateau overlooking the city of Bolzano, the Adler Lodge Ritten is an eco-hotel in Italy's Dolomites. The resort is comprised of two guest buildings with junior suites and 20 lakeside chalets. Inspired by the surrounding forest, the cabins are made from natural materials including sustainably harvested wood. Their minimalist forms resemble the area's alpine shelters while the dark facade fades into the surrounding trees. Interiors are clad in natural timber and feature private bio saunas and floor-to-ceiling glazing highlighting the surrounding landscape. Aside from lodging, the property offers a main lodge with a bar, restaurant, and spa as well as two saunas nestled in the forest. Guests are also encouraged to take full advantage of the surroundings by exploring the mountains and meadows on foot, bikes, or skis.

Photos: Adler Resorts

    Santa Monica Proper Hotel

    Within walking distance of the Pacific Ocean, Santa Monica Proper Hotel is the first luxury boutique to open in the west LA beach town in over a decade. Its 271 rooms and suites inhabit a 1920s Spanish Colonial Revival that's been renovated with a contemporary eye. Warm, sandy hues are adorned with mid-century-inspired furnishings and artwork by local artists to create a calming refuge with expansive views of the coastal scenery. Its rooftop features the only indoor-outdoor pool and lounge in the neighborhood. Overlooking the ocean, the bistro serves dishes inspired by Mediterranean and Californian fare while the on-site restaurant and lobby lounge offer Latin-inspired seafood and small bites paired with small-batch mezcals. Guests can unwind with a therapeutic massage at the Surya Spa or take full advantage of the seaside locale and catch some waves with their in-house surfboard rental.

    Photos: Proper Hotels

    Maison de la Luz

    Atelier Ace brings a bit of Southern Charm back to the trendy warehouse district with Maison de la Luz. The 67-room guest house is located in a 1908 building and aims to offer a private refuge in the Big Easy. its interior is filled with local antique pieces, bespoke furniture, hand-embroidered linens, and marble bathrooms alongside eclectic details like serpent shower handles and custom tiger rugs. Guests can partake in daily tea and coffee service at the lounge or enjoy craft cocktails from the guests-only bar housed in the former City Hall library. If one tires of the quiet delights, each stay also includes access to the neighboring Ace Hotel's rooftop pool and Three Keys music venue.

    Photos: Stephen Kent Johnson / Maison de la Luz

    There's nothing quite as rewarding as a sip of your favorite spirit after a long day in the outdoors, and High Camp Flasks make it easy to enjoy that beverage.     Hawkeye House

    Just 15 minutes from Joshua Tree National Park, the Hawkeye House is a desert dome in California's Yucca Valley. The newly remodeled curved cabin is secluded within 122 acres of mountains and barren wilderness. Featuring a kitchen and two bedrooms, white walls washed in light from the central skylight and reclaimed barn wood give the interior an airy, bohemian vibe that encourages guests to slow down and recharge. On the exterior, an expansive terrace lets you take in the vast landscapes during an al fresco dinner or gaze at the stars from below a timber pergola.

    Soori Bali Resort

    Hidden between Mount Batukaru and terraced rice fields, the Soori Bali Resort is a tranquil refuge on the island's south-west coast. Owner and architect Soo K. Chan creates a serene atmosphere by taking cues from the surrounding environment while prioritizing sustainability. The property consists of 48 minimalist villas made from locally-sourced materials and artisanal decor. Each one has its own private infinity pool offering views of either the mountains or the volcanic black sand beach. A pair of on-site restaurants serve local and international cuisine using ingredients from the resort's garden and local farms. To complete the holistic experience, spend a day at the nearby Tanah Lot or indulge in a Balinese massage at the spa.

    Photos: Soori Bali

    Book Now / $700+

    Salzburg Solar A-Frame House

    Combining environmentally low-impact technology with high-impact design, the Salzburg Solar A-Frame House is an eco-friendly option in Salzburg. The holiday home was originally built in the 1970s. A black facade and timber screen entry gives the exterior a modern look but the biggest update isn't that obvious. Solar modules mounted on the sloped roof supply not only enough energy to power the home, but can also charge an electric car. Within its minimalist white interior, insulation reduces heating and cooling needs while clay boards cool the inside in the summer and store heat in the winter. The spacious ground floor houses the main living area with sleeping lofts above. Just outside a wall of glazing, a wooden terrace overlooks the Chiemsee Alps.

    Photos: Thomas Ziesel / Holiday Architecture

    Sister City Hotel

    Ace is branching out in the Big City with another New York hotel. Considered a spin-off, Sister City enlists Dieter Rams' "less, but better" philosophy for a simplified stay in Manhattan's Bowery neighborhood. Their guest rooms combine Finnish saunas, bento boxes, prehistoric cave dwellings, and the silence of John Cage's 4'33" to create 200 simplified retreats, minimally adorned with custom Italian furniture, warm, neutral hardwoods, and soft Noguchi lanterns. This stripped-down approach aims to encourage guests to leave their rooms and explore the area's museums, restaurants, and cafes. Also in the building are a ground floor restaurant and Last Light — a rooftop bar serving cocktails, local drafts, and unobstructed views of the city.

    Photos: Sister City Hotel
